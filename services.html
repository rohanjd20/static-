GENERAL MEDICINES

A generic drug is a prescription pharmaceutical made to be identical to a brand-name drug that has already been approved for marketing. The properties in terms of dose form, potency, mode of administration, quality, performance characteristics, and intended use are identical. They are marketed under various brand names, generic medications will share the same active ingredients as their name-brand counterparts. The same active components are present in identical concentrations in both generic and brand-name drugs. A pharmaceutical business that finds and commercializes a novel drug and receives a 20-year patent is the one that owns it at the time of its initial development.
